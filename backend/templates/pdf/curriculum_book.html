ACADEMIC PREAMBLE

As an autonomous engineering college affiliated with the University of Mumbai, Fr. Conceicao Rodrigues College of Engineering is dedicated to engineering excellent learners, innovators, and future technical leaders. Our autonomy enables us to dynamically respond to technological shifts and design responsive curricula that balance theoretical foundations with experiential design, modern tools, and professional ethics.

This curriculum structure represents a unified framework curated in collaboration with academic advisors from premier institutes (IIT/NIT/VJTI) and leading technical firms. Emphasizing Test-Driven Design (TDD), advanced software engineering, robust mathematical models, and project-based validation, we ensure our graduates excel across global engineering platforms.

Vision of the Department:

To be a leading center of excellence in engineering education and research, fostering industry-ready technical innovators, ethically sound professionals, and empathetic citizens.

Mission of the Department:

  • To deliver standard-aligned engineering education using student-centric pedagogy and state-of-the-art laboratory environments.
  • To foster research, technical incubation, and industry-institution partnerships for solving contemporary, high-impact societal challenges.
  • To cultivate life-long learning habits, teamwork capabilities, and professional ethical standards.

CURRICULUM ARCHITECTURE & NEP ALIGNMENT

The "H-Tree" Curriculum Philosophy:

Our curriculum operates on the proprietary H-Tree Curriculum Philosophy:
1. The Roots: Deep mathematical and scientific foundations (Basic Sciences & Humanities) ensuring logical clarity.
2. The Trunk: Sturdy, uncompromising core competency (Professional Core subjects) providing structural stability.
3. The Branches: Specialization options, project-based electives, and interdisciplinary choices (Open Electives & Minor streams).

ANNEXURE A: ACADEMIC POLICIES

1. Evaluation Framework:

The academic evaluation uses a two-pronged strategy: Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) representing 40% marks, and End Semester Examination (ESE) representing 60% marks. Students must secure at least 40% in ESE and an overall average of 40% to pass the course.

2. Grading System:

Under the autonomous framework, grading follows a standard relative system converted to a 10-point scale CGPA. Semester grade sheets list both Letter Grades (O, A, B, C, D, E, F) and grade points earned.

3. Syllabus Revision Protocol:

Major syllabus revisions occur every 3-4 years to absorb technological shifts. Minor course corrections or elective introductions can occur annually with Board of Studies (BoS) recommendations and Academic Council approval.
